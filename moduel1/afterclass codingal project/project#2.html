Mini Biography: Muhammad Iqbal

Muhammad Iqbal was a philosopher, poet, and politician in British India. He is widely regarded as having inspired the Pakistan Movement.

Early Life

He was born in Sialkot, a city in present-day Pakistan, and showed a passion for poetry and philosophy from a young age.

Note: Students can adjust details based on the personality they choose.

Key Contributions

Iqbal was a visionary thinker who emphasized self-realization and spiritual revival.

He is known as the Spiritual Father of Pakistan for his profound impact on the nation’s ideological foundation.

One of his most inspiring works was his poem 'Lab Pe Aati Hai Dua Ban Ke Tamanna Meri,' which inspires my teacher deeply because it emphasizes hope and devotion in everyday life.

Correction Corner

He only worked as a poet. He was also aphilosopher, politician, and a key figure in the cultural movement of his time.

Words That Inspire

He once said, ko kar buland itna ke har taqdeer se pehle, khuda bande se khud poochhe, bata teri raza kya hai.

This line encourages us to elevate ourselves so much that even fate asks us what we desire.

My Reflection

I admire Muhammad Iqbal because his words give me strength and remind me—and my teacher—of the power of self-belief.
